# How the Universe Began: Big Bang, Inflation, and the First Structures

The modern origin model of the universe combines several phases: a hot dense beginning, rapid inflation, particle formation, atom formation, and the gradual rise of stars and galaxies.

## Big Bang: What It Means

The Big Bang is not an explosion inside pre-existing space. It is the expansion of space itself from an early hot and dense state.

## Inflation

Inflation proposes a very brief period of extreme expansion in the early universe. It explains the large-scale uniformity of the cosmos and the tiny fluctuations that later grew into galaxies.

## From Plasma to Atoms

As the universe cooled, protons and electrons combined to form neutral atoms. Light could then travel freely, leaving the cosmic microwave background as a key observational signature.

## First Structures

Gravity amplified small density differences over time. Gas collapsed into the first stars, galaxies, and eventually the web-like large-scale structure we map today.

## Conclusion

The origin story is strongly supported by observations, but details remain open—especially around inflation and the earliest moments beyond current measurement limits.
