# Simulation Theory and Information Theory: Are We Living in a Programmed Universe?

Simulation theory is compelling because it reframes an old philosophical question in modern computational language: is experienced reality the deepest layer of reality?

## Bostrom's Trilemma

Nick Bostrom’s argument proposes that at least one of three claims must hold: advanced civilizations usually go extinct, they rarely run ancestor simulations, or we are likely inside one. This is a philosophical probability argument—not an empirical proof.

## Information Theory: Useful but Limited

Shannon’s information theory quantifies transmission and encoding efficiency. It does **not** establish metaphysical claims about whether reality is simulated.

## Frequent Misunderstanding

People often treat "information-like structure" in physics as direct evidence for simulation. That leap is not scientifically warranted.

## Conclusion

Simulation theory is best read as a productive thought experiment: valuable for sharpening assumptions about consciousness, computation, and reality, but far from settled fact.
