# The Cosmic Web: The Invisible Architecture of the Universe

When we look at the night sky, we see stars and sometimes galaxies. What we do not see directly is the large-scale scaffold that organizes them.

Cosmology describes this scaffold as the **cosmic web**: filaments, nodes, and enormous voids. Galaxies are not randomly distributed; they tend to form and cluster along this hidden structure.

## What the Cosmic Web Is

The web is shaped mainly by dark matter and gravity. Ordinary matter falls into those gravitational lanes, forming galaxy clusters and long filamentary bridges across cosmic distances.

## Why It Matters

Understanding the web helps scientists test how structure evolved, how dark matter behaves, and how dark energy affects expansion over time. It turns “empty space” into an active part of the cosmic story.

## Conclusion

The cosmic web changes our perspective: the universe is not a loose collection of objects, but a deeply connected architecture where visible matter follows an invisible order.
