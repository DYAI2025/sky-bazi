# Space Weather Explained: Solar Storms, Geomagnetic Storms, and Real-World Effects

Space weather describes changing conditions in near-Earth space driven mainly by solar activity. It is not speculative: it can affect satellites, navigation, radio communication, and power infrastructure.

## Solar Flares and CMEs

Solar flares release intense radiation. Coronal mass ejections (CMEs) launch plasma clouds that can collide with Earth’s magnetosphere and trigger geomagnetic storms.

## Why It Matters on Earth

Strong storms can disrupt GPS accuracy, degrade high-frequency radio links, and increase risk for spacecraft electronics. In severe cases, induced currents can stress power grids.

## Forecasting and Preparedness

Space agencies monitor the Sun continuously to improve warnings. Better forecasting reduces risk, supports operators, and protects critical systems.

## Conclusion

Space weather is a measurable physical environment. Understanding it is essential for a technology-dependent civilization.
